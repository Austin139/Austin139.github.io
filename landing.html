My journey choosing Computer Science and how I ended up at the University of Limerick

As a kid I’d stand beside my dad, a computer engineer, fascinated by what he was doing. That early spark grew into a passion for computing and set me on the path I’m on today. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Turning curiosity into a path

I’m now a first-year Computer Science student at the University of Limerick. I love building small projects, learning core CS concepts, and exploring how software and data can solve real problems. This page shares the key steps of my journey — from that first spark to the goals I’m working toward today.

How It All Started

My dad often worked from home on weekends. As a curious seven-year-old, I asked, “Can we play games on this?” He eventually showed me how to start the computer, and from that moment my interest kept growing. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Choosing Computer Science

After 10th grade, I treated the choice seriously and committed to Computer Science — a field where my curiosity had already taken root. When my parents asked what, specifically, I wanted to become, I discovered Data Science — a blend of programming, mathematics, and statistics that turns raw data into insight. That combination matched my strengths and interests. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

My Vision and Goals

I want to apply data science to real-world problems — from improving healthcare to tackling environmental challenges and beyond. Studying at UL gives me the foundation and community to grow the skills and network I’ll need to make a meaningful impact. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

What I’m working on next

I’m focusing on core CS modules, small Java/Python projects, logic and binary practice, and building strong study habits. I’ll keep documenting my progress — and I’m excited to share projects, notes, and lessons learned along the way.
